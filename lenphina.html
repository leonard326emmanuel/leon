Welcome to The Lenphina Project Website

Lenphina school was completed in August 1930, for the purpose of educating the first Africans. The school was named after Lenphina Leon, a renowned African-American poe. African-American children in first through ninth grades attended Lenphina school until 1951, when de jure segregation was eliminated from the school systems of Arizona. When segregation in Arizona was eliminated, Lenphina school became the non-segregated John Spring Junior High School, and continued as such until 1982 when the school was closed permanently.
